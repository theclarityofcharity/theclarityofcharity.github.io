Our Story — Jennifer Coburn, Founder & Executive Director — Alberta, Canada

Built by someone who refused to be silenced.

Our founder is a child survivor herself — not of trafficking, but of exploitation and sexual assault. The darkness found her early, and found her more than once. It knew who she was long before she did.


She carried those wounds long into adulthood.


Her personal healing journey only really began when it was triggered — by going deep down the rabbit hole. Following reports of child exploitation, trafficking, sacrifice, and severe abuse. Horrors she recognized from the inside. What began as a search for healing became a spiritual battle.


She wasn’t supposed to know. But she did. And she couldn’t shut up.


What she found, she spoke. The world — not yet ready to hear it — called it conspiracy. She can somewhat empathize with their fear, their denial. It is easier to disassociate from a truth this heavy than to face it. But she was awake. And she stayed awake. With the price tag of persecution.


Shunned. Ostracized by friends, community, and family alike for daring to name what others refused to acknowledge. Branded a conspiracy theorist for findings the world is only now catching up to.


Trauma steals one’s voice.


In the years of isolation that followed, she turned the weight of what she carried into knowledge — studying child psychology, trauma-informed care, and somatic healing. Learning from leading experts including Gabor Maté, Peter Levine, and Diane Langberg. Doing the deep work. Healing. Building the foundation that would become this organization.


At times she withdrew — nearly abandoning what she believes is her soul mission and the building of this organization because of it.


She didn’t.


While struggling to find support at home — because so much of this was still buried in silence and denial — she was already giving internationally, supporting and learning from numerous survivor-focused nonprofits across the globe. She carried this knowledge largely alone, for years, while the world slowly, painfully caught up.


What was done in the dark is finally coming to light — for healing.
The Clarity of Charity is to become what this last decade of standing alone was always manifesting — for the children of North America.


The darkness always knew who she was.
Now the hunted has become the hunter.

The Critical Gap We Are Closing

To rescue a child is a victory. What comes next is the war.

A defeating reality is the return of many survivors back into situations of exploitation following aftercare services — because resources are limited, and corruption or abuse can sometimes be present in shelters, foster care, and government services. These displaced children find themselves back in an at-risk situation once more.

“Walking with survivors is incredibly challenging. It’s complex, costly work that takes years, not days — not a program, but a childhood rebuilt from the ground up.”
  • Major shortage of appropriate safe housing for trafficked children across North America
  • Many survivors re-enter exploitation after aftercare ends — resources simply run out
  • Corruption and abuse present in existing shelters and foster care systems
  • Our injured young ones will never relate to a “normal childhood” — they need communities built for them
  • No single modality treats the full complexity of extreme childhood trauma
  • Current systems fail to provide the trauma-informed care these children critically need

Why This Must Exist

These children will grow up together — in healing.

In reality, these injured young ones will never be able to relate with a “normal childhood” or healthy children whose innocence remains intact. That is not a tragedy to hide — it is a truth to honour.


So we build for them. Communities designed specifically for them. Trauma-informed. Low stimuli. Set in natural environments. Where children who have survived the same darkness can heal together, grow together, and reclaim together what was taken from them.


Giving them the care and the tools needed to walk into their own life with healing, confidence, and a future. That is true freedom.


“The body heals with play. The mind heals with laughter. And the spirit heals with joy.”

The Healing Sanctuary

Imagine a place where the echoes of pain are replaced by laughter.

We have found it. Tucked within vast, unspoiled nature — open skies, towering trees, and the quiet sounds of the earth healing itself. Far from the chaos that harmed these children. Far from the noise, the triggers, the danger. A place where the environment itself begins the work of restoration before a single program begins.


This sanctuary will be designed to hold our first community of survivors — dozens of young souls who will live, learn, grieve, laugh, and heal together. A large main residence with everything needed to raise children properly: shared meals prepared in a proper kitchen, a library filled with books, open spaces for movement and play, homeschooling programs, and a uniquely designed indoor therapeutic playroom — because all children deserve a childhood.


Staffed 24/7 with professionals and experts in their field — trauma-informed medical care, education, and therapy around the clock. This is costly, complex work — and it is worth every cent.

How We Heal

Understanding the multidimensional needs of young survivors of extreme trauma.

Trauma destroys. It fragments the soul. Multidimensional healing restores. Band-Aid approaches — a prescription, a six-session cap, a single modality — do not restore what was taken. Our programs address the whole child: mind, body, and spirit.

🧠

Mind

Mental health care must be addressed with deep intention and expertise. Our approach centres on:

  • Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioural Therapy
  • Complex Trauma & Extreme Sexual Abuse care
  • Helping children understand their pain — not carry it blindly
  • Showing what care and health actually look like
  • Making lasting change possible, not just crisis management
🪴

Body

Medical care is a critical component of survivor care. Our children arrive carrying physical injuries no child should bear:

  • Poverty, abuse, and malnutrition-related illness
  • Infection and untreated conditions
  • Internal injuries from repeated abuse
  • STIs, pregnancies, and reproductive trauma
  • Advanced restorative healing programs as a priority investment

Welcomed with unconditional love and acceptance. Compassionate healers creating a secure environment where children will be cherished and nurtured — whilst on their empowering healing journey.

  • Connection and belonging as medicine
